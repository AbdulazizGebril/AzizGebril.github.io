World Migration By Income Regions



The above pie chart illustrates the migration destinations represented by income regions as defined by the UN Department of Economic and Social affairs. Around 65% of migrants by 2020 lived in regions classified as high-income regions. In addition, it is also noticed that as the region income decreased, the migration volume decreases as well.


GDP Per Capita 1990-2019

The plots below illustrates the GDP Per Capita ($) through the period 1900-2019 for top migration destinations and origins. It is observed that contrast between Top migration destinations and migration countries, where the top migration destination have much higher GDP per capita compared to top migration origins. The Russian Federation is common between both, From the data, the migration from the Russian Federation was the highest from the period etween 1990-2000 where the GDP Per Capita is at its lowest compared to the following years.


Poverty Rate (% Population) 1990-2019

The plots below illustrates the Poverty rate (population %) through the period 1900-2019 for top migration destinations and origins. The poverty rate is defined as percentage of population that earn less than 5.50$ per day. It is observed that contrast between Top migration destinations and migration countries, where the top migration destination have much lowerr poverty rate compared to top migration origins. Same for the GDP Per Capita analysis, The Russian Federation's poverty rate as the highest between 1990-2000, and a significant drop can be noticed prior 2000.


Unemployment Rate (%) 1990-2019

The plots below illustrates the Unemployment rate (%) through the period 1900-2019 for top migration destinations and origins. In contrast to previously discussed economic indicators, the Unemployment rates for top migration destinations and origins is quite similiar. In fact, the highest unemployment rate through all countries analysed is Spain. However, Its imperative to note that unemployment rate also is related to population. Most migration origin countries are highly populated countries, In addition unemployment rate does not represent pay as seen from the analysis of the poverty rate.
